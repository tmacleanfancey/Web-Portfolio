What We Believe

01

Empathy Before Assumptions

We never assume we know how users think or what they need. Every project starts with listening — through interviews, surveys, and observation — before we open Figma.

02

Design Is a Team Sport

Our best ideas came from disagreeing, debating, and combining perspectives. No one person owns the design — we all own the outcome together.

03

Iterate Without Ego

We critique the work, not the person. When user testing reveals we were wrong, we treat that as progress — not failure. Every round of feedback made our designs better.

The best design decisions we made weren't made alone — they were made in a room where everyone felt safe enough to say "I think we got this wrong."

— Team Reflection · INFO 360 · 2025
03

How We Worked Together

Our Collaboration Approach

Across two full design projects — SafeClick and FamilyJam — we developed a rhythm that kept research, design, and communication moving in parallel rather than in sequence.

"We didn't divide the work and disappear. We divided the lead, then collaborated on everything."

Each member had a primary role, but no section of the design was owned by just one person. We reviewed each other's work regularly and brought honest critique to every session.

01

Discover Together

We ran research as a team — attending interviews together so all three of us heard user insights firsthand, not secondhand through a summary.

02

Sketch Individually, Decide Together

Each member sketched solutions independently, then we compared ideas and built a single direction by combining the strongest elements.

03

Prototype in Parallel

While one member built screens in Figma, others prepared test scripts and materials — keeping momentum without waiting for handoffs.

04

Test & Debrief as One

All three team members participated in every user test. We debriefed immediately after, capturing observations while they were fresh.

05

Reflect Honestly

After each project phase we asked: what did we learn, what did we miss, and what would we do differently? These reflections shaped our next iteration.
